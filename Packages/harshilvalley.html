Harshil Valley - An Unexplored Valley

03 Days 02 Nights
Best Season Winter (Nov, Dec, Jan) Spring (Feb, March, April) Summer (May, June, July)

Harsil: The Himalayan Paradise Located in the Uttarkashi district of Uttarakhand, India, Harsil is a picturesque town, vacationer slope station, and military base situated on the banks of the Bhagirathi River, en route to Gangotri, a revered Hindu pilgrimage site.

Key Facts:

  • Elevation: 9,005 ft (2,745 meters) above sea level
  • Distance from Uttarkashi: 78 km
  • Distance from Gangotri National Park: 30 km
  • National Park Area: 1,553 square km

Notable Features:

  • Indigenous habitat
  • Renowned apple production
  • Scenic beauty amidst the Himalayas
  • Bhagirathi River flowing through the town
  • Proximity to Gangotri, a significant Hindu pilgrimage site

Geographical Significance:

Harsil lies in the lap of the Himalayas, offering breathtaking views and a serene atmosphere, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers, trekkers, and those seeking spiritual enlightenment.
