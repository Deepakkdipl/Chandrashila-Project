CHAR DHAM YATRA 2024 UTTARAKHAND

10 Days 09 Nights
Best Season Summer (May, June, July) Monsoon (Aug, Sept, Oct) November
Uttarakhand, a state in northern India, is renowned for its numerous ancient temples and shrines. With a rich history of gods and goddesses, many pilgrims visit various destinations to pay respects and seek blessings. The Char Dham sites, situated in Uttarakhand, are commonly referred to as the 'Land of God'. The Char Dham route is the most popular among all holy sites and is on the list of many devotees. Millions of devotees travel to these four holy places every year. The term 'Char Dham' was coined by Jagat Guru Shri Adi Shankaracharya, meaning 'four abodes of gods'. These holy pilgrimage sites are believed to help attain salvation and self-knowledge. Initially, the Char Dham Yatra proceeded from west to east, starting from Yamunotri, then Gangotri, Kedarnath, and finally Badrinath. The Char Dham Yatra involves driving and trekking, with an option for helicopter travel. We offer various Char Dham packages for 2024, including tours from Delhi, Ahmedabad, Haridwar, Bangalore, Dehradun, Rishikesh, and Hyderabad. Here's an overview of the four Char Dham temples:

1. Yamunotri Temple:

  • Located in the Garhwal Himalayas at 10,800 feet
  • Dedicated to Goddess Yamuna
  • Situated amidst beautiful mountain peaks and the sacred Yamuna River
  • Features a black marble idol of Goddess Yamuna

2. Gangotri Temple:

  • Situated at 10,200 feet in the Himalayan Range
  • Dedicated to Goddess Ganga
  • Located amidst beautiful mountains and the sacred river Ganga
  • Originates from the Gangotri glacier at Gaumukh

3. Kedarnath Temple:

  • Located near Mandakini River in the Garhwal Himalayan Range
  • Situated at 11,760 feet
  • Worships Lord Shiva as the 'Lord of Kedar Khand'
  • Originates from the Gangotri glacier at Gaumukh

4. Badrinath Temple:

  • Situated along the banks of Alaknanda River in the Garhwal Hills
  • At a height of 10,280 feet
  • Dedicated to Lord Vishnu
  • Features a black stone statue of Vishnu

We offer packages for each temple, including Char Dham hotel booking from Haridwar, Delhi, and Dehradun.

Barkot, a picturesque town nestled on the banks of the Yamuna River, serves as a gateway to the revered Yamunotri Dham. The town boasts stunning Himalayan vistas and is surrounded by lush green forests, making it a nature lover's paradise. The temperature in Barkot typically ranges from 64°F to 83°F (18°C to 28°C). Day 2 - Barkot – Yamunotri - Barkot (36km drive & 6km Trek /each side)

Bus volvo
Stay not Applicable
  • Activities: Today, after an early morning breakfast, drive to Janki Chatti, the starting point of the Yamunotri trek. From Janki Chatti, you have three options to reach the Yamunotri Temple:
    1. Horse ride
    2. Palki (palanquin)
    3. Trek

The Yamunotri Temple, dedicated to the Goddess, features a stunning Black Marble Idol and is situated in the western region of the Garhwal Himalayas at an altitude of 3,291 meters (10,797 ft). The temple was built in 1839 by Sundarshan Shah, the king of Terhi Gadwalat at that time.

    Visit Surya Kund, a sacred spot where you can boil potatoes and rice in the hot water springs and consume them as prasad (offerings).
