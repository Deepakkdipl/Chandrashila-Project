YULLA KANDA

YULLA KANDA

03 Days 02 Nights
The Yulla Kanda trek is a spiritual and a trekking route in the Spiti Valley region , that takes you through the picturesque Rora Valley in Kinnaur District. Starting from Yulla Khas, a small village renowned for its Kinnauri apples, the trek winds its way through scenic landscapes to the sacred lake with the world's highest lord Krishna's Temple. This route offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural richness, and mythological significance, making it an unforgettable experience.

Highlights

- Enchanting views of Kinnaur mountains

- Ancient Lord Krishna Temple inside the lake

- Highest Krishna temple in the world

- Cultural richness of Yulla Khas village, famous for Kinnauri apples

- Mythological significance: Believed to be created by the Pandavas during their exile in the Himalayas

Keep in mind that the Yulla Kanda trek requires a good level of physical fitness and acclimatization to high altitudes. It's essential to plan carefully, hire experienced guides or join a reputable trekking group, and follow all safety protocols.

Who should attend?

  1. Spiritual seekers: Those interested in exploring sacred sites and experiencing the spiritual energy of the Himalayas.
  2. Nature lovers: Individuals who appreciate scenic landscapes, mountains, and lakes.
  3. Adventure enthusiasts: Trekking enthusiasts looking for a moderate challenge with rewarding views.
  4. Cultural explorers: Those interested in learning about Kinnauri culture, traditions, and mythology.
  5. Photography enthusiasts: The trek offers stunning views, making it an ideal destination for photography.
  6. Individuals seeking solitude: Those looking for a peaceful, serene environment to connect with nature and themselves.
  7. Trekkers with moderate experience: Individuals with some trekking experience, looking for a new challenge.

However, the trek may not be suitable for:

  1. Beginners: The trek involves moderate difficulty, steep inclines, and high altitude.
  2. Those with health concerns: Individuals with health issues, such as heart problems or breathing difficulties, should consult their doctor before embarking on the trek.
  3. Families with young children: The trek may be challenging for young children due to the terrain and altitude.

It's essential to assess your physical fitness, experience, and interests before joining the Yulla Kanda trek.
